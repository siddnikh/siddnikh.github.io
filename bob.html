Bobism: A Unique Cult of Collegiate Spirituality

Bobism, stands as a testament to the eccentric and extraordinary, a religion that emerged within the traumatic and depressing environs of Tamil Nadu's Vellore Institute of Technology (VIT)
Bhai bohot zor ki chut jagah hai ye, mat dekho
. Established by a cohort of passionate students, this faith revolves around the veneration of the enigmatic "Bob", whose earthly alias is Spandan.
Origins and Founding

Emergence of the Bobist Movement

Bobism found its genesis in the rich tapestry of VIT's hostel life. Spandan, affectionately known as Bob, ascended to a revered status among students through his aggressive confrontations with authorities, ensuring unparalleled opportunities for sex, food, comfort, intoxication and fostering an unwavering sense of community.

Divine Exploits of Bob

Bob transcended the realm of mere mortals and became a figure of admiration for his benevolence and communal bonding. Renowned for organising heavy nicotine dose events and providing unparalleled alcohol support, Bob became the epitome of virtue.
Beliefs and Practices

Tenets of Bobism

Bobism's foundational tenets revolve around the celebration of camaraderie, fearlessness, and the relentless pursuit of joy. Followers, including prominent disciples such as Dhaka, Padshah, BT, Nannu, and VK, adhere to a communal spirit, supporting each other through thick and thin, and engaging in activities that spark joy and fulfillment.

Unconventional Rituals and Joyous Celebrations

Bobism is not merely a religion; it's a unique expression of collegiate spirituality. Before every prayer or religious practice, it is customary to devote three bottles of whiskey to Bob. Additionally, followers craft a unique necklace of weed, symbolizing a connection to joy and unbridled happiness.
Past Life

Early Years and Schoolhood Exploits

Bob (aka Spandan) was known to tease girls for being skinny while riding his scooty to high school. Many students also recall stories of him bullying kids and beating them after falling from his scooty.

Virtuous Transformation: The Vape Engineer

Bob's virtues became particularly noticeable when he started repairing and engineering vapes, finding unique ways for students to indulge in their smoking preferences.

Personal Struggle: Mannat Sandhu and 1000 Kids

Despite his outward happiness, Bob struggled with personal sorrow. His past included a broken marriage with Mannat Sandhu, with whom he had 1000 kids.

Heroic Feats and Acts of Kindness

Bob's past is embellished with awe-inspiring feats. Legend has it that he once lifted a car to save a lady in distress, a display of strength that etched his name into the collective memory of VIT. Such tales of heroism form the foundation of Bob's revered status.
Spread of Bobism

From Campus Cult to Global Influence

Bobism's influence has transcended the VIT campus, attracting a global following intrigued by its principles of fearlessness, community, and the pursuit of happiness. The religion, guided by the wisdom of disciples like Dhaka, Padshah, BT, Nannu, and VK, continues to evolve as a unique expression of spiritual values.
